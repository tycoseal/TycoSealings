Metallic Ring Type Joint Gaskets(RTJs)

Metallic Ring type Joint gaskets are used in high pressure applications for exploration and production in Oil and Gas Industry.

A gasket is used to create and retain a static seal between two flanges which connect a series of mechanical components while containing a wide variety of fluids (liquids and/or gases). These static seals aim to provide a complete physical barrier against the fluid contained within and block any potential leak paths through gasket material or between surfaces of flange and gasket, while still being capable of being disassembled and reassembled.

Type R | OVAL Type R | Octagonal

The R-Type ring gaskets are manufactured in two shapes, Oval or Octagonal. Both shapes have the same pitch diameter as the flange groove they communicate to. These gaskets are used in pressures up to 10,000 PSI. The octagonal type has a higher sealing co-efficient than the oval, therefore it is the most desired gasket shape. The oval type is the only gasket that will fit a bottom radius groove. Both the Gaskets can be used in Flanges with flat bottom v-grooves. OVL and OCT is used as abbreviation for Oval and Octagonal shapes respectively.

Type RX

RX gaskets are compatible to R Type Oval or R type Octagonal rings used on API 6B flanges. Thickness or the height of the these RX type of gaskets are more than R type gaskets, hence the weight of one ring is also more than R type gaskets. RX type gaskets are best suitable to replacement in the areas where R type gaskets are used under high pressure flow applications like on shore under Sea applications.

Type BX

BX gaskets are designed for pressures up to 20,000 PSI. These gaskets are compatible to R Type Oval or R type Octagonal rings used on API 6BX flanges. The virtual face-to-face contact of the flange faces is facilitated by BX gasket that results in the gasket to be fully confined on both the inner and outer diameters. A pressure balance hole is incorporated in BX gaskets that ensure equalization of pressure, which that is possibly generated in the grooves.

Spiral Wound Gaskets

Spiral wound gaskets are designed and produced in manner to even recover under fluctuating loads during process pressure and temperature variations. A pre-formed metal winding strip combined with layered compressible material is used as sealing. The compressible material is allowed to fill the imperfections in the surfaces of the flange in which the gasket is used for sealing. Mechanical resistance is provided by the Outer metallic strip that holds the filler material.

Rubber gaskets

Modified PTFE gasket, though with a tendency to creep under excess load, can be used as an excellent gasket material with certain limitations due to the inert nature to many chemicals. PTFE is used to produce soft gasket with higher strength. The combination of chemically inert PTFE along with stress retention property results in reduction of the negative effect of PTFE on the mechanical stability in joints.
